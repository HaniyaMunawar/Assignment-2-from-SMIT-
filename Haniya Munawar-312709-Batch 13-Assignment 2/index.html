Five notable software companies:

1. Microsoft

History: Microsoft founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen. Microsoft began with a vision to place a computer in every home.
Its first major breakthrough was an operating system (MS-DOS) which was licensed to IBM for its personal computers. Microsoft
later launched Windows, a graphical extension for DOS, which became the dominant OS in the world.

Industry Impact: Microsoft revolutionized personal and enterprise computing with its Windows operating system and Office software.
Azure is now a major player in the cloud industry, and its acquisitions (e.g., LinkedIn, GitHub) have further strengthened its foothold
across various sectors. Microsoft is also pushing into AI with the integration of large language models into its products.

2. Apple

History: Apple founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. Apple began with the release of the Apple I computer.
The company's fortunes rose with the success of the Macintosh in 1984. After a challenging period in the 1990s, the return of
Steve Jobs revitalized the company with the introduction of iconic products like the iPhone, and iPad.

Industry Impact: Apple has been a trendsetter in personal electronics and mobile computing, with the iPhone redefining the smartphone market.
Its integration of hardware and software is unmatched, and Apple continues to set industry standards for user experience, design, and innovation.

3. Amazon

History: Amazon founded in 1994 by Jeff Bezos as an online bookstore, Amazon quickly expanded into a wide range of e-commerce categories.
It launched Amazon Web Services (AWS) in 2006, which would go on to revolutionize the cloud computing industry. Today, Amazon is a leader
in e-commerce, cloud computing, and digital services.

Industry Impact: Amazon revolutionized e-commerce with its user-friendly platform, logistics, and fulfillment services. AWS is a pioneering force in
cloud computing, offering scalable infrastructure that powers businesses across all industries.

4. Google

History: Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin as a search engine. Its mission was to organize the world's information and make
it universally accessible. Google rapidly expanded beyond search into online advertising, mobile, and cloud computing.

Industry Impact: Google transformed how people access information online through its search engine, advertising platform (AdWords), and YouTube. Android dominates
the global smartphone market, while Google Cloud is a major player in cloud computing.

5. Adobe Inc

History: Adobe was founded in 1982 by John Warnock and Charles Geschke, initially focusing on the PostScript page description language, which revolutionized desktop publishing.
Adobe expanded into multimedia and creativity software, with its breakthrough product being Photoshop, released in 1990. Over the years, Adobe has built an extensive range of
creative and digital marketing solutions.

Industry Impact: Adobe has had a massive impact on the creative industry, becoming synonymous with professional design and multimedia tools. Photoshop, Illustrator,
and Premiere Pro are standards in their respective fields. Adobe shift to a subscription-based cloud model (Creative Cloud) revolutionized software distribution.
